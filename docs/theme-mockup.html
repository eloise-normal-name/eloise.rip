getting comfortable with chest resonance after six months

voice practice transition

Six months ago I couldn't reliably hit the same note twice. Now I wake up and the first thing I do is hum, just to check in — to feel where my voice is sitting. It's become a small ritual, like checking the weather.

The thing nobody tells you about voice training is that the goal isn't a destination. You're not working toward a fixed target. You're building a relationship with something that changes day to day based on sleep, hydration, stress, the humidity in the room. What you're actually practicing is attention.

The voice you hear in a recording is never quite the voice you remember making. Learning to close that gap takes longer than any exercise.

what actually helped

Consistency mattered more than duration. Fifteen minutes every morning was more effective than an hour twice a week. The muscle memory seems to consolidate overnight — I'd go to sleep frustrated and wake up having quietly improved.

Recording everything, even when I didn't want to listen back. The record button is the most honest mirror.
